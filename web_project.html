The Founding Father of Pakistan

"I do not believe in taking
the right descion, I take

Education

After getting primary education from Sindh Madrasa-tul-Islam, Muhammad Ali Jinnah went to England
for higher education where he got his degree from Lincoln's Inn in law and become a top lawyer.

Pakistan Movement

Muhammad Ali Jinnah joined Muslim League in 1916. Then he started struggle for a seprate
homeland of Muslims. He succeded and Pakistan came into existence in 1947.

1945 Elections

1945-46 general elections were very important for partition of united India and formation of Pakistan. Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah led the Muslim league to victory in elections.

Pakistan Movement & Quaid-e-Azam

19 Points Formula

In 1929, Quaid-e-Azam in response of Ghandi's points, presented his 19 points formula.

The Pakistan Resolution

In 1940, at annual meeting of Muslim league in leadership of Muhammad Ali Jinnah, Pakistan resolution was passed which gave Muslims a goal to fight for.
